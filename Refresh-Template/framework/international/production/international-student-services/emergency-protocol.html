Emergency Protocol

Life-threatening Emergencies

If you are on campus, call the CWRU Police Department at 216.368.3333.

If you are off campus, call 911.

Examples of life-threatening emergencies:

  • Car accident
  • Criminal activity
  • Missing persons
  • Suicidal thoughts
  • Homicidal thoughts
  • Natural disasters (tornado, flood, etc.)
  • Sexual assault
  • Other violence

Immigration-related Emergencies

Between 8:30 a.m. and 5 p.m. on weekdays, call ISS at 216.368.2517.

Outside of regular office hours, call the CWRU Police Department at 216.368.3333, and the dispatcher will help connect you an ISS staff member.

Examples of immigration-related emergencies:

  • Detainment at US port of entry
  • Immediate threat of deportation

Non-Emergencies

Examples of non-emergency needs for assistance:

  • Culture shock
  • Stress
  • Conflicts with classmates or professors
  • Financial problems
  • Scams
  • Identity Theft
