Lu-Jean Feng, MD

The Lu-Jean Feng Clinic, Health and Wellness Center (Cleveland, Ohio)

Feng, founder and medical director of The Lu-Jean Feng Clinic, is a board-certified plastic and reconstructive surgeon with more than 30 years of experience.

She graduated from Yale University School of Medicine and did her residencies in general surgery at University of California Hospitals, San Francisco, and in plastic surgery at University of North Carolina. In addition to her Microvascular Surgery Fellowship at New York University Medical Center, she has been a clinical instructor of plastic surgery at New York University School of Medicine. Her specialties include cosmetic surgery of the face and breasts, painless abdominoplasty (tummy tuck), and natural breast enhancement with regenerative stem-cell-enhanced fat.

Feng is board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery as well as a member of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery, the American Medical Association, the American Society for Reconstructive Microsurgery, and the Ohio Valley Society of Plastic Surgeons. As well as being on the International Visiting Committee at Case Western Reserve, she has been a clinical associate professor at the School of Medicine.

Lu-Jean Feng is the founder and medical director of the Lu-Jean Feng Clinic and is the only plastic and reconstructive surgeon on staff. She has more than 30 years of experience and is recognized on a global level.

While she specializes in cosmetic surgery of the face, breast and body, Feng is recognized as being one of the first to use autogenous tissue for breast reconstruction, which gained national attention in the 80s. She is also one of few surgeons to perform an all-natural breast enhancement using a patient’s own regenerative stems cells and fat. And, most recently, she introduced a “painless and drainless” abdominoplasty (tummy tuck).
